## Why Nigerians Are Shipping More from China

China is Nigeria's largest trading partner, supplying everything from electronics and clothing to machinery, cosmetics, and construction materials. The price advantage of buying directly from Chinese manufacturers or wholesale markets like Yiwu International Trade Market, Guangzhou Baiyun Leather Market, or Shenzhen electronics hubs is significant — but so is the complexity of getting those goods from the factory floor to your warehouse in Lagos or Onitsha.

Most importers run into problems not with sourcing but with logistics. Unreliable freight agents, surprise costs at Nigerian customs, goods held indefinitely at Apapa Port, and poor cargo handling during the long ocean transit are the common failure points. Getting the logistics right starts with understanding your options.

## Air Freight from China to Nigeria

Air freight from China to Nigeria takes 7 to 14 working days from collection to delivery. Cargo departs from Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport, Shanghai Pudong, Beijing Capital, or Shenzhen Bao'an Airport and arrives at Murtala Muhammed International Airport in Lagos. Onward delivery to other Nigerian states — Abuja, Port Harcourt, Kano, or Onitsha — typically takes a further 2 to 3 working days after Lagos arrival.

**Air freight suits** smaller consignments, high-value goods such as electronics or jewellery, time-sensitive stock like fashion items ahead of a sales season, and any shipment under 100kg where the speed premium is commercially justified. It is not practical for furniture, machinery, or bulk wholesale goods where weight and volume would make the air freight cost prohibitive.

Air freight is priced on whichever is greater — the actual weight of your cargo or its volumetric weight. Volumetric weight is calculated as Length × Width × Height in centimetres, divided by 5,000. A large but lightweight box can cost significantly more than its physical weight suggests. Contact J3Cargo for a current per-kilogram rate from your specific Chinese origin city.

## Sea Freight from China to Nigeria

Sea freight from China to Nigeria takes 45 to 60 days from port of loading to delivery in Lagos. Container vessels depart from Guangzhou Nansha Port, Shenzhen Yantian or Shekou terminals, or Ningbo-Zhoushan Port — China's busiest — and typically tranship at ports in Singapore, Malaysia, or the Middle East before arriving at Apapa Port or Tin Can Island Port in Lagos.

**Sea freight is best suited for** wholesale stock in large volumes, heavy or bulky goods such as machinery, generators, furniture, and building materials, any shipment above 100 kilograms where cost per kilogram is the primary concern, and full container loads for businesses that import regularly.

You can choose between a **Full Container Load (FCL)** — where you occupy an entire 20-foot or 40-foot container — or a **Less than Container Load (LCL)**, where your cargo shares container space with other shippers and you pay only for the cubic metres your goods occupy. LCL is the practical starting point for growing importers who do not yet have the volume to fill a container on their own.

### Documentation and Pre-Shipment Compliance

Shipping from China to Nigeria requires a commercial invoice, packing list, and a bill of lading (for sea) or air waybill (for air). Commercial importers must also process a **Form M** through a licensed Nigerian bank before the goods arrive in Nigeria — this is a mandatory pre-import declaration that funds the transaction through official channels. For regulated product categories — including cosmetics, food products, health supplements, and certain electronics — a **SONCAP certificate** confirming compliance with Nigerian standards, or a **NAFDAC registration number**, may be required before clearance. Confirm the documentation requirements for your specific goods with our team before your goods are shipped.

## What You Can and Cannot Ship from China to Nigeria

Most manufactured goods and wholesale merchandise can be shipped from China to Nigeria. Commonly cleared categories include new electronics and smartphones, clothing and shoes (new items only), household goods and kitchenware, solar panels and renewable energy equipment, cosmetics and beauty products with valid NAFDAC numbers, and industrial machinery and agricultural equipment.

**Items that cannot be shipped** include counterfeit branded goods of any kind, second-hand clothing and textiles (which are outright banned from Nigerian import), certain restricted food and beverage products, narcotics, and weapons or ammunition. Nigerian customs at Apapa and Tin Can Island actively screens for counterfeits — goods with fake brand markings are routinely seized and destroyed. Always source from legitimate suppliers and ensure packaging accurately reflects the actual product.

## Customs Duty on Goods from China

Goods arriving in Nigeria from China attract import duty calculated on the CIF value — the cost of goods plus insurance plus freight. Duty rates vary by product classification under the Nigerian Customs tariff schedule, generally ranging from five percent for raw materials to thirty-five percent or higher for finished consumer goods. VAT at 7.5 percent applies in addition to import duty on most categories.

As of September 2025, Nigeria's de minimis threshold means goods valued at $300 USD or below may be exempt from import duty. For commercial shipments above this value, factor in duty cost before placing your order — the difference between a twenty percent and thirty-five percent duty rate on a large consignment is significant, and accurate budgeting at the sourcing stage prevents cash flow problems at the port.

## Common Mistakes Nigerian Importers Make When Shipping from China

**Undervaluing the commercial invoice** to reduce customs duty is the most common and most costly mistake. Nigerian customs officers are trained to identify goods with suppressed declared values, and the consequences — cargo seizure, port holds lasting weeks, and fines that exceed the original duty — are far more expensive than honest declaration.

**Skipping quality inspection** when buying from new or unverified Chinese suppliers on Alibaba or Aliexpress is how importers end up receiving containers full of goods they cannot sell. For any order above £1,000, request samples first. For significant volumes, engage a third-party inspection service in China to verify quality before the goods leave the factory.

**Ignoring regulatory documentation** for controlled product categories is how consignments get destroyed at the port. NAFDAC requirements for cosmetics, food products, and health items are non-negotiable. Confirm what your product needs before you pay the supplier.

## FAQ

### How long does sea freight from China to Nigeria take?

Sea freight from Guangzhou, Shenzhen, or Yiwu to Lagos typically takes 45 to 60 days, including ocean transit and customs clearance at Apapa or Tin Can Island Port in Lagos. Delivery to states beyond Lagos takes a further 2 to 5 working days depending on the destination.

### How long does air freight from China to Nigeria take?

Air freight from China to Lagos takes 7 to 14 working days from collection at the Chinese supplier to arrival in Lagos. Delivery to other Nigerian states takes an additional 2 to 3 working days after Lagos arrival.

### What documents do I need to import goods from China into Nigeria?

You need a commercial invoice, packing list, and a bill of lading or air waybill. Commercial importers must also process a Form M through a Nigerian bank before the goods arrive. Regulated goods such as cosmetics, electronics, and food products may additionally require a SONCAP certificate or NAFDAC registration number.

### Can J3Cargo consolidate goods from multiple suppliers in China?

Yes. When you negotiate FCA terms with each of your suppliers, they deliver directly to J3Cargo's China hub. We consolidate all goods into a single outbound shipment regardless of how many suppliers are involved. ### Should I use LCL or FCL for my first import from China?

LCL is the better choice for a first-time importer. You pay only for the cubic metres your cargo occupies in a shared container, with no minimum volume. As your order sizes grow and you begin to fill more than half a container consistently, switching to FCL becomes more cost-effective per kilogram.
